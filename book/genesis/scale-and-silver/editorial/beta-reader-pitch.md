# Beta-Reader Pitch — *A Bond of Scale and Silver*

> Standalone adult romantasy · ~154,000 words · by Post Peleos
> Spoiler-light recruiting pitch (stops before the climax). For the spoiler-complete
> version see `synopsis.md`; for the sales blurb see `back-cover.md`.

---

**She was born to be a secret. The world is about to make her a war.**

Amelia has never been allowed outside. She is the first vampire born — not made — in a
thousand years: her mother the Queen's cherished, forbidden secret, a girl carrying blood
magyk that shouldn't exist, kept behind a bolt because her very existence could shatter the
treaty holding three peoples apart. She's been loved fiercely and caged completely her whole
life, and she wants exactly one thing the world will never allow her — to be *seen*.

Then a man poisoned by an old grief unveils her before both courts, and the war begins.

On the run at last, Amelia crosses paths with Korvan — the shifter chief's outcast son, shamed
by a change that never came, until the first full moon of his life makes him the one creature
more hunted than she is: a dragon out of dead legend, a clan of one, no elders, no traditions,
the loneliest thing alive. What should be a gift out of myth is a catastrophe — now she is
unmistakably the witch, and he the monster the propaganda promised.

Thrown together on a brutal road with the war closing behind them, two hunted things learn each
other. What grows between them isn't fate and isn't rescue — it's a choice, made by two people
with nothing to inherit it from, in a world doing everything it can to break them apart. And
being finally seen is going to cost Amelia everything she loves.

*A dark, adult romantasy of dangerous devotion, chosen love, and the terrible price of the truth.*

---

## What you'd be reading

- **Genre / vibe:** Adult epic-political fantasy romance, dual POV (Amelia & Korvan). Anne
  Bishop's *Black Jewels* for voice and the dangerous-devoted dynamic; Carissa Broadbent and
  Danielle L. Jensen for the romantasy shelf.
- **Tone:** Slow-burn, forced-proximity, spare and intense rather than flowery. Menace by
  restraint. Bittersweet, earned ending — not a fairy tale.
- **Heat:** Explicit/spicy, earned in the back half (two open-door scenes). **No sexual
  violence** — the book deliberately cuts away from the worst.
- **Content notes:** war and battlefield violence, on-page grief and loss, an intense
  controlling-but-loving parent thread.
- **Length:** ~154,000 words. Standalone — complete arc, no cliffhanger.

**If you like:** hidden royalty, one-of-a-kind shifters, two outsiders who choose each other, a
villain who can't be bought, and a heroine who is dangerous rather than rescued — I'd love your
eyes on it.
